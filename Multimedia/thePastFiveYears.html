How have changes in web technology shaped or influenced web development and design?

Timothy Berners-Lee proposed and implemented the system that became the World Wide Web in 1989 to facilitate communication between researchers. He also designed the first Web browser. The first website was put up at CERN in France on 6 August 1991. It gave information on creating webpages and about the use of hypertext (Wikipedia, 2013). Since then there have been many developments that have influenced the practice of web design.

The invention of tables led to the development of table based page layout, something that tables were never intended for and which still causes controversy today. Still the use of tables caught on with a vengeance because it opened up the possibility of designing the visual structure of a page. There are still many web pages based on tables and designers who feel that for some purposes tables still have their use (Budd, 2004). The people on the other side of the fence point to the fact that the same effect can be accomplished with CSS, with the advantages of much smaller code and the ability to change the appearance of all the pages on a website in a uniform manner by merely changing the code in a single CSS file (Keith, 2003).

Shockwave and Flash are two competing technologies developed for showing movies and animation on the Web. Shockwave has a much faster rendering engine than Flash but has no plugin available for Linux or Solaris and has come to be most used in game development (Wikipedia, 2013). Flash has good compatibility across browsers but has the disadvantage of requiring the installation of the Flash player. Flash also creates difficulties with SEO and accessibility with mobile devices such as iPhone and Blackberry (New Jersey web design agency, 2013).

Dynamic HTML or DHTML allows the qualities of an HTML tag to be changed by an event such as a mouse click, date or time and thus a page that interacts with the viewer. DHTML has four parts which make it work the document object model or DOM, scripts either JavaScript or ActiveX, CSS and XHTML (Kyrnin, 2013).

3DML is a language that was invented in 1996 by Michael Powers. Used for 3-D websites and with a non-valid XML, it requires a plugin for IE, Netscape Navigator, the AOL browser, and Opera. Development stopped in 2005 and there is no plugin for Firefox (Wikipedia, 2013).

What changes have occurred in the past 5 years in web design due to the expansion of the Internet?

In the past five years the growth of the online retailing sector of e-commerce has increased the need for interactive database driven websites to track and display merchants' goods and to take the customers orders. Small and medium sized enterprises have also increased their use of the Internet to order goods and services from other businesses and to make payments. This leads to the need to design websites that facilitates and automates these business to business interactions (Commission, 2011).

The expansion of the Internet into the field of education in the past few years has provided a large new market for the services of web designers who are needed to design new interactive websites such as the Canvas website used at Bellevue College to coordinate the online learning courses of the school. The need for adult career retraining has helped to spur the growth in online enrollments and 69 percent of academic leaders think student demand for online courses is growing and most believe their online enrollments will increase in the coming year(2007) (Seaman, 2007).

While there will be a greater need for the design of more complex websites predesigned solutions are gaining ground in the field of small site design. With WordPress it was possible for anyone to design their own site. Although this was possible it turned out that many people didn't want to be their own website designer. They turned to the free themes available for WordPress. This didn't make the designers any money but they used free themes to draw customers to their websites and made their money by selling to customers who had special needs and were willing to pay for custom themes (Turnbull, 2011).

Have these been major changes or more subtle?

The growth of the online retailing sector of e-commerce has increased in many fields and consumer satisfaction is greater with online retailors than with the traditional bricks and mortar stores. (Dusto, 2013) The trends toward progressive enhancement, responsive design, native support for plug-in features, the increased use of apps and the explosion of devices used, all point to a sea change in web design and development. To meet the demands of their online retailers, developers and designers will have to embrace the new standards and technologies so the websites will display properly on the new tablets and mobile devices, and they won't lose customers who increasingly try to access them from these devices (Grannell, 2012).

The expansion of the Internet into the field of education in the past few years has provided a large new market for the services of web developers and designers who can design web systems that can fulfill the needs of schools their online students demand. Microsoft's website lists 32 Microsoft approved partners who can provide the expertise to build these systems (Microsoft, 2013). This is just the tip of the iceberg with many companies around the world entering the field.

Since WordPress was released in 2003 it has been downloaded over 65 million times. The free, open source blogging software is the most popular blogging software on the Web and has spun off a whole industry devoted to the development of custom WordPress themes, plugins and WordPress user services (Wikipedia, 2013).

What have been the largest challenges in providing users with content on the Web for your sector?

In the late 1950s the Internet began as a file sharing system to allow U.S. Government researchers to share files. Originally patterned after the academic model of typewritten pages it was not until the introduction of Mosaic with the use of hypertext and hyperlinks that the website model was born. Websites are classified by the type of content they contain and generating content is an important part of the work of building a website (Wikipedia, 2013 ).

The challenge of providing content varies with the type of website. But the first rule must be that the content must be relevant to the purpose of the website. There are many ways of generating content. One way is a blog. Blogs short for web log, may be the output of one individual or may solicit questions or comments from visitors. Some websites solicit short articles from writers whos reward is a link back to his own web page. Much content is the result of paid writers. The written word is not the only type of content. Content may consist of video, audio and images. You tube is a huge website consisting of video clips ranging from short user produced instructional videos to complete movies. Many musicians have their own websites which promote and sell their own art. The same in true for visual artists.
