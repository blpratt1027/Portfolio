Site One - Seattle Art Museum

The Seattle Art Museum Website has an extremely organized and efficient use of space. The site has a simple yet useful design, with a tranquil color scheme. The top of the home page offers a search field to search the entire site. Below it is a large advertisement link which rotates through three images advertising just a few of the exhibitions available now or future dates. Below the ads are the main menus which are Art, Visit, Programs, Calendar, Get Involved, Membership, and About SAM. Calendar and Membership are links to their own pages, while the other menus have drop-down sub-menus. On the left of the home page appear photo links as well as hours of operation for the SAM, Seattle Asian Art Museum, and the Olympic Sculpture Park. On the right of the home page appear word links to Tickets, Get Enews (SAM newsletter by email), SAM Gallery, School Tours, Video (a SAM video on YouTube), and Blog (SOAP which has much more detail about everything available at the SAM). In the center of the homepage are links under a heading titled “What's Happening at SAM” with logo links to Facebook, Twitter, subscribe to Enews, as well as a Share + icon.

A sub-menu of Art, called Collections, shows an in depth way of searching through the museum's art collections. When you click on Collection Highlights, you are offered brilliant thumb-nail photo links to each collection area showing a sample of the collection in that area. This is a very useful way of giving the site navigator a way of seeing what type of collections are available without having to view each one at a time. The samples of Art shown as thumbnails are able to be viewed in larger detail with the Adobe Flash Player Plug in. These samples are uploaded onto the site as a .jpg file.

On almost every page there are links to Facebook and twitter. The Video link takes you to YouTube where the featured video playing is Meet the Characters of “Rembrandt, Van Dyck, Gainsborough.” There are many videos you can choose from to view which tell about each exhibition as well as different aspects of the SAM. This is very easy for the navigator to use and view the videos since it takes them straight to a video supported site instead of adding this multimedia to their site directly.

One of my favorite links on the site is a sub-menu, under the About Sam menu, called SAM Goes Green. I found this part of the site to be really interesting as they talk about their commitment to sustainability and offer a link to a video showing how they use green gardening techniques at the Olympic Sculpture Park. They also go into detail about the relationships they have formed with different organizations to help support and accomplish the goal of staying green. Furthermore, they have listed resources and ways of getting involved and helping out with this goal, as well as links to all of the organizations who are partners' in the environmental sustainability and educational efforts.

The SAM website is definitely using the most accessible technology to date and is able to reach its maximum amount of viewers based upon the technology they are using. They have kept the site very user friendly, simple yet pleasing to the eye, organized with detailed menus, and many links to social networks. I think they are reaching the highest level of viewers from their many search options on their site, Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube accounts. This is a site I will be adding to my “likes” list, and can definitely see myself visiting this site for personal reasons in the future.

Site Two - Learn ASP.NET 4

Learn ASP.NET 4 is a website I was referred to by an instructor in one of my courses. The site offers free tutorials in ASP.NET and C# programming.

The site offers text books available for purchase from Amazon and Barns and Noble. The books are also available for download in PDF format directly from the site so to use the PDF version you need Adobe PDF viewer which in available free from Adobe. Most computers have the viewer installed but if it isn't already installed it is easily downloaded from the Adobe website. To view the free videos requires the Adobe Flash Player. It is also available free on the Adobe site.

The courses are very complete and include step by step setup instructions and email support. (Smart, 2012) I was so impressed that I bought the basic course and the text from Amazon. I am working my way through the videos and these people really know their stuff. It occurred to me that their website should reflect that, so I took a look at their site source code. I have never seen cleaner code. I particularly liked the way they divided up their CSS into several files according to the function of the code. I didn't know you could do that.

PDF stands for Portable document format. It is software, hardware and operating system independent. This means that it displays the same under all conditions. It was an Adobe Systems proprietary format until Adobe released it as an open standard in July 2008. Anyone can create royalty free applications for it. (Wikipedia, 2013)This makes it an extremely popular format and most computers come with a reader for it installed.

Adobe Flash Player supports vector and raster and 3D graphics. It is freeware used for viewing multimedia and streaming video and audio from a web browser with a browser plugin. There is also support for mobile devices for it and it can be run directly from an operating system. Flash player is very popular and is installed on over ninety percent of PCs with an Internet connection. (Wikipedia, 2013)
